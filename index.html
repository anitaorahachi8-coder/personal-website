Drake (Aubrey Drake Graham)

Aubrey Drake Graham (born October 24, 1986) is a Canadian rapper, singer, and actor. He is credited with popularizing R&B sensibilities in hip-hop music through rap-singing. Drake first gained recognition as Jimmy Brooks in the CTV teen drama series Degrassi: The Next Generation (2001–2008) and began his music career by independently releasing the mixtapes Room for Improvement (2006), Comeback Season (2007), and So Far Gone (2009) before signing with Young Money Entertainment.

Top 5 Drake Albums

Take Care(2011)

Widely considered Drake’s masterpiece, this album solidified his signature "emotional" sound. It features a groundbreaking blend of moody R&B and high-level rap, with iconic tracks like "Marvin's Room," "Headlines," and the Rihanna-assisted title track. It remains his highest-selling project to date, certified Diamond (10 million units) in the US as of late 2025.

Nothing Was The Same(2013)

Often praised for its conciseness and consistent vibe, this album is frequently cited as "Peak Drake". It balances aggressive hype anthems like "Worst Behavior" and "Started From the Bottom" with smooth R&B hits like "Hold On, We're Going Home". Many critics consider it his most coherent body of work.

If You're Reading This It's Too Late(2015)

This surprise-released mixtape is the "holy grail" for fans who prefer Drake as a pure rapper. It moved away from radio-friendly melodies toward paranoid, aggressive trap production on tracks like "Energy" and "Know Yourself". Despite being billed as a mixtape, it moved over 535,000 units in its first week.

Views(2016)

Though divisive upon release for its 80-minute length, Views has aged into a commercial behemoth. It captures the global, "taste-making" era of Drake, heavily influenced by dancehall and Caribbean sounds. It contains his most streamed and commercially successful singles, including "One Dance" and "Hotline Bling".

So Far Gone(2009)

The breakout mixtape that launched his career is still heralded for its historical significance. It established the blueprint for the modern "singing rapper" and remains a fan favorite for its vulnerable storytelling on tracks like "Houstatlantavegas" and "Successful".
